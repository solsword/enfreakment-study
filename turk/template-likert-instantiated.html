Preliminaries

This HIT is part of an MIT scientific research project, and should take about 25–35 minutes to complete (there are 125 survey questions plus 14 exit questions). Your decision to complete this HIT is voluntary. We will not collect identifying information about you beyond your worker ID, which we will not retain in stored copies of the data that we gather. The only information we will have, in addition to your responses, is the time at which you completed the survey. The results of the research may be presented at scientific meetings or published in scientific journals. Clicking on the 'SUBMIT' button at the bottom of this page indicates that you are at least 18 years of age and agree to complete this HIT voluntarily.

You may only complete one HIT from this survey. If you complete multiple HITs from this survey, your subsequent HITs will be rejected.

To provide useful data for this survey, you must be at least 18 years old and able to read English and clearly view the images on this page. If you don't meet these criterion, please click 'Return HIT' to avoid being rejected.

To confirm that you meet these criteria, please write the word “yes” in the box below.

Survey Instructions

You will be presented with five fighting game characters, including three images, a country, a quote, and a short bio for each character. Please indicate your agreement (or disagreement) with each of the statements listed for each character using the following scale (the 25 statements are the same for each character, and the scale is repeated throughout the survey for reference):

  1. Strongly disagree
  2. Disagree
  3. Somewhat disagree
  4. Neutral
  5. Somewhat agree
  6. Agree
  7. Strongly agree

Make sure you also answer the final exit questions after completing all 5 character sections, and remember that your answers are anonymous and will be used for scientific purposes only. This survey should take you about 25–35 minutes to complete.

Note: Each question will change color once you provide an answer to help make it obvious what you've already finished. There are no right or wrong answers.
